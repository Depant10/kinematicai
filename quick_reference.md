# Quick Reference: Home Language Support

**Print this page and put it on your fridge!**

This is your daily cheat sheet for supporting communication. Remember: connection and fun are more important than perfectly speaking words.

### The "OWL" Strategy
When you are playing with him, remember to **OWL**:
*   **O - Observe:** What is he looking at? What is he doing with his body?
*   **W - Wait:** Stop talking. Count silently to 10. Give him time to process and initiate.
*   **L - Listen:** Pay attention to any sounds, gestures, or words he uses.

### Core Techniques
1.  **Follow His Lead:** Play with what he wants to play with. If he wants to line up cars, line up cars with him.
2.  **Parallel Talk:** Be a sports commentator. Narrate what *he* is doing. ("Wow, fast car!")
3.  **Self-Talk:** Narrate what *you* are doing. ("I am washing the cup. Wash, wash.")
4.  **Expansion:** Add one word to what he says. If he says "Juice," you say "More juice!"
5.  **"Follow-In" Commenting (For Verbs):** Say the action word *while* he is doing the action. If he is jumping, say "Jump! Jump!"

### Handling "Lost in His Own World"
*   **Do not force interaction.** He may be regulating his nervous system.
*   **Gently join in.** Sit near him and do the same activity he is doing quietly.
*   Wait for him to look at you or gesture before demanding language.

### Teaching Prepositions & Verbs
*   **Make it physical:** You cannot teach "in" or "run" with flashcards. He needs to put the toy *in* the box and physically *run*.
*   **Use routines:** Talk about what you are doing during daily routines (cooking, bathing, getting dressed).

### Remember: What NOT to do
*   **Don't test him.** Stop asking "What's this?" and "What color is that?"
*   **Don't withhold things.** Don't hold his favorite toy hostage until he says the word for it.
*   **Don't force eye contact.** He can hear and learn without looking you in the eye.
*   **Don't suppress stimming.** Flapping, spinning, and echoing are ways he regulates and communicates. Accept them!

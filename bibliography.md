# Bibliography — Autism Expressive Language Home Support Plan

Sources organized by evidence tier.

---

## Tier 1: Meta-Analyses and Randomized Controlled Trials

1. **Schreibman, L., Dawson, G., Stahmer, A. C., et al. (2015).** Naturalistic developmental behavioral interventions: Empirically validated treatments for autism spectrum disorder. *Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders, 45*(8), 2411–2428. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25737021/

2. **Network meta-analysis of parent-mediated NDBIs (2024).** 32 RCTs comparing PRT, ESDM, ImPACT across language, social, and parent fidelity outcomes. *PMC, 2024.* https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/

3. **Meta-analysis of ESDM (12 studies, N=640).** Significant language improvements (g=0.408). Includes Waddington et al. and related reviews. *Various journals, 2016–2023.*

4. **Kasari, C., et al. — JASPER RCTs (2006–2022).** Multiple randomized controlled trials demonstrating joint engagement mediates language outcomes. Sample sizes N=15 to N=113. *Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders; JCPP; others.*

5. **Shih, W., Shire, S., Chang, Y.-C., & Kasari, C. (2021).** Joint engagement is a potential mechanism leading to increased initiations of joint attention and downstream effects on language. *Autism Research.* https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/

6. **Kaiser, A. P., & Roberts, M. Y. — EMT systematic reviews and RCTs (2011–2024).** Enhanced Milieu Teaching: caregiver-implemented, generalization across settings. *ASHA journals; others.*

7. **Carter, A. S., Messinger, D. S., Stone, W. L., et al. (2011).** A randomized controlled trial of Hanen's "More Than Words" in toddlers with early autism symptoms. *Journal of Child Psychology and Psychiatry, 52*(7), 741–752. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21969874/

8. **PRT efficacy studies (multiple RCTs, 2006–2024).** Pivotal Response Treatment: functional utterances, vocabulary, generalization. *Various journals.* See also: What Works Clearinghouse review.

9. **Parent-mediated intervention meta-analyses (2020–2024).** Multiple systematic reviews showing moderate effects on social interaction, mixed on language. *Frontiers in Psychiatry; Research in Developmental Disabilities; others.*

10. **Systematic reviews of AAC in autism (2014–2024).** Consistent evidence AAC does not delay speech; may facilitate spoken language. *Augmentative and Alternative Communication; JSLHR; others.*

11. **NDBI + AAC combination review (2024).** NDBIs combined with AAC produce larger language improvements for minimally verbal children. *PMC, 2024.*

## Tier 2: Other Peer-Reviewed Studies

12. **Milton, D. E. M. (2012).** On the ontological status of autism: The "double empathy problem." *Disability & Society, 27*(6), 883–887. https://doi.org/10.1080/09687599.2012.710008

13. **Crompton, C. J., Ropar, D., Evans-Williams, C. V., Flynn, E. G., & Fletcher-Watson, S. (2020).** Autistic peer-to-peer information transfer is highly effective. *Autism, 24*(7), 1704–1712. (Empirical support for double empathy problem.)

14. **Spatial language acquisition in ASD (multiple studies, 2010–2023).** Peaks and troughs in preposition mastery; caregiver input predicts growth. *Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders; Developmental Psychology.*

15. **Verb deficit literature in autism (2005–2023).** Social cue dependence, morphosyntactic subgroup, follow-in input effectiveness. *Frontiers in Psychology; Journal of Speech, Language, and Hearing Research.*

16. **Alexithymia in autism (2013–2024).** 50–85% prevalence; "alexithymia hypothesis"; visual supports and body awareness interventions. *Molecular Autism; Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders.*

17. **Screen time and language development (2016–2024).** Displacement of conversational turns, passive viewing effects, fast-paced content concerns. *JAMA Pediatrics; Pediatrics.*

18. **Prizant, B. M., Wetherby, A. M., Rubin, E., & Laurent, A. C. (2006).** The SCERTS Model: A comprehensive educational approach for children with autism spectrum disorders. *Paul H. Brookes Publishing.* (Foundational framework with peer-reviewed components.)

19. **GLP/NLA systematic review (2024).** No published peer-reviewed intervention studies found. *Perspectives of the ASHA Special Interest Groups / related.*

## Tier 3: Clinical Guidelines and Professional Body Recommendations

20. **ASHA — Autism Practice Portal.** Evidence-based practice guidelines for SLPs working with autistic individuals. https://www.asha.org/practice-portal/clinical-topics/autism/

21. **NICE — CG170: Autism spectrum disorder in under 19s: support and management (2013, updated).** Play-based social-communication interventions. https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg170

22. **AAP — Autism Identification, Evaluation, and Management.** Universal screening, early intervention, medical home. https://www.aap.org/autism

23. **RCSLT — Autism Clinical Guidance.** Evidence-based, shared decision-making, person-centered practice. https://www.rcslt.org/

24. **RCI (Rehabilitation Council of India).** Statutory registration for rehabilitation professionals. CRR verification. https://rehabcouncil.nic.in/

25. **ISHA (Indian Speech-Language and Hearing Association).** Professional standards, clinic accreditation, locate-professional tools. https://ishaindia.org.in/

26. **AAP — Media and Children Guidelines.** No screens under 18 months; limited co-viewed content 2–5. https://www.aap.org/
